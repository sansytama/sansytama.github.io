**The title was an excerpt of thoughts and dialogs that pop up when I practice zazen. I was supposed to only observe, let it come and let it go. But in mean time when my practice is impure, and my mind untrained, I would like to record some of them to reobserve and reflect when I've got the chance.**

--------------------

<br>

After reaching a certain amount of time, I find myself no longer serve much purposes. The feeling of wanting to run away from it all arises.

I don't know why. Even I don't want that. But I can't help but doing that. Looking back again, I ask myself if there's any event I would like to change, anyone I'd wish to reconnect again, any regrets, any opportunities. The answer is still no, it has been that way since the darkest time, it's still the same answer now.

There's nothing to change, not that it's about the possibilities of given second chances, it's more about why? Why change? Why now? Why me? What is there to gain? Who am I if one thing from the past happened differently? Would it be someone happier or would it be another I typing all these wandering words? Would the I that knows me know the I that knows me that knows me? Would he knows that I'm thinking about him without knowing him?

Would he pities my situation or would he be happy for me?

    I came from brilliancy.

    What is this?

What is this that keeps talking? 

When I ask, it responds. When I reach out, it runs away. When I wonder, it's by my side. The more I seek, the more vague it becomes. It makes me feel familiar yet... unfortunate, even though I'm not quite sure what it is....

What is this?
